This project aims to create an Arduino robotic arm that can be remotely controlled. The robotic arm is powered by an Arduino Mega 2560 and uses 3 micro servos, IR, and an Arduino remote. The primary objective of the robotic arm is to grab or throw objects. However, the robotic arm can be further improved by adding sensors or a machine learning program. For instance, you could add sensors to detect the distance between the robotic arm and the object it needs to grab or throw. Alternatively, you could use machine learning algorithms to train the robotic arm to recognize specific objects and perform specific actions based on those objects.

This project aims to create an Arduino smart plant pot that can water itself on a schedule while ensuring stable water, humidity, temperature, and light conditions. The smart plant pot uses a plastic lid that has LED lights, a photoresistor, and fan, an Arduino Mega 2560, a stepper motor, a 28BYJ-48 stepper motor (with ULN2003A driver board), an RTC module, LCD, buttons, DHT22 humidity/temperature sensor, water sensor, plant pot, straw, string, half-cut bottle, and an Arduino UNO. To improve the smart plant pot’s capacity to hold water and soil for plants, you could consider using a larger plant pot or adding an external water reservoir. Additionally, you could use a more powerful pump to increase the amount of water the smart plant pot can carry.
